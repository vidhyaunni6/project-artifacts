Agentic AI for Autonomous POI Validation & Map Edits

An autonomous first-pass map reviewer that reads driver-submitted storefront photos, cross-references Grab's Places database, auto-merges the confident majority, and routes only the hard cases to human operators.

01 · Problem

Manual map review is the bottleneck

GrabMaps depends on crowdsourced data — millions of driver-partners submit photos, new Points of Interest (POIs), and business updates through the app and KartaCam.

Today, a human operator must open each submission, read the storefront, check it against the existing base map, and decide whether to add it, update it, or discard it. That work is:

🐌

Slow & labor-intensive

Every edit waits in a human queue, so the map lags behind Southeast Asia's fast urban change.

💸

Expensive at scale

Review hours grow linearly with submission volume across every market.

🔁

Mostly routine

The large majority of submissions are clear-cut — humans spend their time on cases a machine could clear.

The core insight: ~80% of submissions are easy and repetitive; the value of a human is in the ambiguous ~20%. The opportunity is to let people spend their time only where judgement is actually needed.
02 · Solution

An AI agent as the autonomous first-pass reviewer

When a driver submits a storefront photo, an AI agent extracts the business details, cross-references them against the Places DB and local web sources, and makes a confidence-based call.

✓

Auto-merge

High-confidence, no conflict → added to the map automatically. No human touch.

⚑

Route to human

Ambiguous → sent to an operator queue with the summary already written for a fast Approve/Reject.

✕

Reject

Unreadable / insufficient signal → discarded, nothing written to the map.

Design principle

The agent is autonomous on the easy majority but never guesses on the hard minority. Humans keep full control of everything ambiguous, and every routing threshold is a dial the operations team can tune.

03 · Workflow

The 4-station agent pipeline

A submission moves through four stations and comes out with a decision plus a plain-English rationale.

StepWhat it doesTechnology
1 · ReadExtracts business name, address, operating hours + a confidence score per fieldVision-Language Model (Claude Opus 4.8 / GPT-4o)
2 · MatchChecks whether the place already exists nearby (new vs duplicate vs hours-change)Dice coefficient (name similarity) + Haversine (GPS distance)
3 · CorroborateAsks local/web sources whether they agree; raises or lowers confidencePlace-search / web lookup
4 · DecideCombines signals into one score and routes by clear rulesTransparent weighted scoring + thresholds

Routing rules (auditable, tunable)

Sign unreadable (<60%)RejectNo reliable data to act on
Strong match to existing placeHuman reviewPossible duplicate, or an hours update
New place, high confidence (≥85%)Auto-mergeSafe to add automatically
New place, medium confidenceHuman reviewWorth a quick human check

Why these choices (the TPM rationale)

  • Vision-Language Model — the only component that can "read" a photo into structured fields; the technology Grab already uses.
  • Real matching math (Dice + Haversine) — the duplicate/new decision is computed, not guessed, so it's explainable and testable.
  • Next.js + React — industry-standard, easy to staff, and lets the UI and the agent's backend live in one project.
  • Leaflet + OpenStreetMap — free, open mapping; no paid map key needed to demo the impact.
  • Transparent rules for the decision — deliberately not a black box; thresholds are dials Ops controls and Audit can inspect.
Prototype vs production — one seam. The vision step is isolated behind a single function, extractFromImage(). The demo runs offline with mocked reads (no key, no cost); going live means replacing the inside of that one function with a real Claude Opus 4.8 vision call. The matching, corroboration, rules and UI stay exactly the same. That's a deliberate decision to de-risk the mock → production transition.
05 · Reuse in any TPM work

A repeatable playbook, not a one-off

Strip out "storefront photos" and "map" and what's left is a general pattern for any high-volume manual review process — content moderation, KYC/document checks, support-ticket triage, invoice/expense approvals, QA review, claims processing.

①

Find the toil

A repetitive, high-volume human decision where most cases are routine. Volume × handle-time = the target.

②

Split easy vs. hard

Insert AI as the first pass. Automate the confident majority; route the uncertain minority to humans. Confidence-based routing is the heart of it.

③

Keep humans on edge cases

Never auto-decide when unsure. Hand humans a pre-written summary so even the hard cases get faster.

④

Instrument & benchmark

Measure automation rate, time saved, and error rate from day one. Pilot in one place, prove the numbers, then scale.

The five questions to ask of any process

1What's the repetitive human decision, and how often does it happen?→ sizes the opportunity
2What inputs does the human look at to decide?→ defines what the AI must read/extract
3What signals separate an easy case from a hard one?→ becomes the confidence/routing logic
4What's the cost of a wrong auto-decision?→ sets how conservative the auto bar is
5How will we measure toil removed and quality kept?→ the metrics that justify funding
The transferable one-liner: "Use AI to clear the routine majority automatically, route the ambiguous minority to humans with the analysis pre-done, instrument the toil saved, and prove it in one pilot before scaling." That sentence applies far beyond maps.

Ambient AI Agent for Field-Force Meeting Toil

01 · The idea

Turn meeting talk into filed follow-ups — automatically

After every adviser–client meeting, someone re-keys the agreed actions into downstream tools. This agent listens, transcribes, extracts the action items with an LLM, and files each one in Jira via function-calling — behind a human confirmation gate, so the adviser stays present and nothing is forgotten.

🎙️

Capture

Records & persists the meeting — never blocks the conversation.

🧩

Understand

Speech → transcript → summary + action items with owners & due dates.

✅

Act

Each item → a Jira issue via tool-calling, behind a confirm gate.

02 · How it works

Three decoupled stages, one durable queue

⛓️

Decoupled & durable

Stages hand off through a SQLite queue — capture never waits on model latency, and a crash just resumes.

🧠

Claude + function-calling

Opus 4.8 extracts the action items and calls the tool that files them in Jira.

🛡️

Safe by default

Every write passes a gate (dry-run / confirm / --yes); idempotency keys stop duplicates.

🔌

Pluggable & reusable

Swap STT / LLM / Jira by config. Same pattern fits any conversation-to-commitment workflow.

One seam demo → production: runs fully offline with LLM_BACKEND=mock + STT_BACKEND=fake (no key, no cost, no tickets). Going live just flips to real Claude + STT — queue, gate and Jira integration stay identical.
